Hotel Xcaret · Dec 26–29
What's included — and what to expect

All-fun inclusive means unlimited access to Xcaret's parks. We have 3 days here. Read what each experience is actually like before voting.

🪂
Xplor Park Included
Ziplines · ATV cave rafts · underground rivers · all ages
▾

Xplor is Xcaret's adventure park — think Indiana Jones meets Cancún. Seven zipline circuits fly you over the jungle canopy (up to 500 meters per run), then you switch to ATVs through stalactite-filled caves with headlamps, and finish in wooden rafts floating through underground rivers. It's fully included in our stay and legitimately great for all ages from kids 9+ through grandparents.

Xel-Há Aquatic Park Included
Natural snorkel bay · lazy river · cliff jump · most relaxing of the parks
▾

Xel-Há is a massive natural aquarium — a protected cove and inlet where you snorkel among thousands of tropical fish from the moment you enter the water. There's a long, winding lazy river to float down, a cliff jump platform (about 10 feet — optional), and hammock areas strung over the water. It's the chillest of the Xcaret parks and perfect for anyone who wants to be in the water without the adrenaline of Xplor. Food and drinks are included.

Xcaret México Espectacular Night Show Included
2-hour cultural spectacular · nightly 7pm · 300+ artists on stage · bucket list
▾

This is the night show that people come back to Xcaret specifically for. Held every night at 7pm in the massive open-air Gran Tlachco Theater, it's a 2-hour journey through 500 years of Mexican history — Mayan civilization, the Spanish conquest, the colonial era, independence, and modern Mexico — told through 300+ performers, regional dances, live music, horses, colorful birds, and incredible costumes. Universally cited as one of the best cultural shows in the entire Caribbean/Mexico region. It's included with our hotel stay — no extra charge. We just need to reserve seats.

"The night show was the highlight of our visit. Xcaret México Espectacular is designed to bring together the history, traditions, music, and cultural diversity of Mexico in a way that leaves a lasting impression." — TripAdvisor reviewer

HA' Restaurant — Michelin Star Dinner Extra cost
9-course tasting menu · ~$246/person · adults only · book immediately at check-in
▾

HA' (pronounced "ha" — it means "water" in Mayan) is the first restaurant at Xcaret to earn a Michelin star, awarded in 2024 and confirmed again in 2025. It's led by Chef Carlos Gaytán, who became the first Mexican chef ever to earn a Michelin star both inside and outside Mexico. The restaurant serves a 9-course contemporary Mexican tasting menu — think wagyu A5, truffled mushroom beignets, Mayan-inspired ceviche, and dishes that change seasonally. The dining room is designed around water — a vortex water feature greets you at the entrance. It's adults only, dress code applies (no shorts, no sandals), and it costs roughly $246 per person. It is absolutely a splurge — but it's genuinely one of the best restaurants in all of Mexico.

"The whole experience at HA' was amazing, start to finish. Changes the definition of food. Service is incredible. Very detail-oriented but not pretentious. Must try if staying at Xcaret." — OpenTable reviewer (4.8 stars, 385 reviews)

Optional Day Trips
Dedicated waterpark day — worth it?

Hotel Xcaret now has 5 on-property waterslides at Casa Vida (added in the 2025 expansion) — fun for younger kids, pool-based. But Aqua Nick and Jungala are in a completely different league: dedicated full-day waterparks with 21 slides, thrill rides, and Nickelodeon theming. The question is whether the hotel slides are enough, or whether a full waterpark day trip is worth the extra cost and drive.

🏊
Xcaret Hotel — On-Property Slides at Casa Vida Included
5 new slides · pool-based · added Summer 2025 · best for ages 9–12
▾

The 2025 expansion added 5 waterslides at Casa Vida — descending from different levels into a pool. This is genuinely new information: Xcaret is no longer a zero-waterslide resort. That said, these are hotel pool slides, not a dedicated waterpark. Reviews describe them as fun for younger kids (especially 9–12 age range) but not the kind of all-day thrill park experience that Aqua Nick or Jungala deliver. Think "really good hotel pool area with slides" rather than "waterpark." The real question is whether that's enough for your family, or whether you want to spend a full day at a proper dedicated park.

Aqua Nick — Nickelodeon Water Park ~$155/person
21 slides · Soak Summit Tower (59 ft) · Sliming events · SpongeBob theming · best for kids
▾

Aqua Nick is Nickelodeon's flagship waterpark in Latin America — 21 slides across 3 zones, with the Soak Summit Tower rising 59 feet and featuring trapdoor capsule drops. There are PAW Patrol and SpongeBob-themed areas, actual Sliming events where characters dump slime on the crowd, and character meet-and-greets. It's the park that's hardest to say no to if you have kids ages 9–15 — the theming alone is a differentiator. About 50 minutes south of Grand Fiesta Americana.

"Big enough park to spend a full day in. Good variety for small kids, medium kids and teens. Only thing that put a damper on things was the water is very cold — but it was so much fun we forgot about it." — Viator reviewer

Jungala Aqua Experience — Vidanta ~$74/adult
Elite thrill rides · AquaLoop 360° · 5-ft wave pool · longest lazy river in Latin America
▾

Jungala is designed for riders who want serious slides — not theming. The AquaDrop is a near-vertical plunge, the AquaLoop is a complete 360° loop (one of very few in Mexico), and the Flatline Loop sends you through a full inversion. The wave pool generates waves up to 5 feet — the biggest in the region. The lazy river is the longest in Latin America. It's low-density by design (Vidanta limits capacity), so lines are almost nonexistent. This is the park for teens and adults who've done Aqua Nick before or want a more intense experience. Closed Thursdays and Fridays.

"Never waited in a line. Tons of chairs available. The lazy river was the highlight. Everything was impeccably clean. I'd skip Xel-Há and head to Jungala." — TripAdvisor reviewer

Optional Add-On Experiences
Isla Mujeres · Chichen Itza · snorkeling · sunset cruise · cenotes · spa
▾

Grand Fiesta is a great base for day trips if anyone wants more than the beach. None of these are required — but they're worth knowing about before you vote.

Isla Mujeres — 20-min ferry, stunning island, best snorkeling near Cancún. Easy half-day.
Chichen Itza — one of the 7 wonders of the world, ~3 hrs each way. Long day but bucket-list.
Sunset cruise — catamaran, open bar, Cancún coastline at golden hour. Typically ~$80/person.
Snorkeling / scuba trip — Cancún Underwater Museum (MUSA) or Isla Mujeres reef. Good for certified divers.
Cenote tour — freshwater sinkholes, crystal clear, different from anything else. Half-day.
Spa day — Grand Fiesta has a spa on-site. Easy to book for adults while kids do the beach.
